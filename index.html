Card One

To be, or not to be, that is the question: Whether 'tis nobler in the mind to suffer The slings and arrows of outrageous fortune,

Card Two

Or to take arms against a sea of troubles And by opposing end them. To die—to sleep, No more;

Card Three

and by a sleep to say we end The heart-ache and the thousand natural shocks That flesh is heir to: 'tis a consummation.

Card Four

To die, to sleep; To sleep, perchance to dream—ay, there's the rub: For in that sleep of death what dreams may come,

Card Five

When we have shuffled off this mortal coil, Must give us pause—there's the respect That makes calamity of so long life.

Card Six

Thus conscience doth make cowards of us all, And thus the native hue of resolution Is sicklied o'er with the pale cast of thought,
